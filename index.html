My Coding Story

I felt accomplised and challenged as a Project Manager. I was managing other professionals', I understood the ins-and-outs of my projects, I was anticipating risks and solving problems. I was a good at my job, but I was unsatisfied.

I had a nagging desire to create, design and 'do' something myself. Coding and app development had always been on my radar but I never seriously considered a career change into Tech.

In June 2016 I took the opportunity of represeting Australia at the Ultimate Frisbee World Championships in London to take a career break and extensively travel Eastern Europe.



*Quarter-life crisis in full swing*



While travelling, I entertained my curiosity in website, application and program development. In September 2016, I settled in Berlin and commited to learn the art and science of Web Developement with the impression that I would teach myself to code.

A few months down the particularly bumpy road I havn't looked back. The 'self taught path' is flexible and liberating but is only as structured as your selfcontrol; theres no assignment or exam pressure (which while isnt pleasureable, undoubtedly works), only your own motivation to make a little progress each day.

Web Dev Notes

I made this website using HTML, CSS and javascript to track my progress and goals, practice the tools I learn and to share my 'Web Dev Notes' with the community.

My vision for 'Web Dev Notes' is to create a collection of tutorials, examples and notes as a reference for myself and others learning Web Development.

Please reach out if you want to talk: code, web-dev, design, my notes, flag any of the endless bugs on my site or ultimate frisbee!
